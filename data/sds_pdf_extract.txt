EXECUTIVE SUMMARY The Makerere University Business School (MUBS) Service Delivery Standards (SDSs) establish a binding commitment of MUBS to operational transparency, quality assurance, and public accountability across its main campus and regional networks in Jinja, Mbale, Mbarara, and Arua. Mandated under the Universities and Other Tertiary Institutions Act, Cap. 262, and explicitly aligned with Uganda's National Development Plan IV (NDP IV), this document serves as a core management and performance tracking engine.  These standards were formulated under the executive steering of an internal Technical Working Group and officially validated by the National Planning Authority (NPA) to mandate measurable delivery baselines for all academic, financial, administrative, and auxiliary operations. Structurally, the service is systematically aligned to the six action-oriented strategic pillars that support the MUBS strategic objectives. Daily operations under these standards are governed by a standardized, user-verifiable implementation matrix that outlines precise deliverables, metric-based performance indicators, and strict benchmarks regulating quality, step-by-step processes, processing timelines, accessibility, and service frequency. To ensure absolute alignment, the Quality Assurance Directorate (QAD) serves as the primary institutional watchdog, maintaining direct accountability for running routine internal compliance audits, deploying digital tracking checklists, and analysing stakeholder feedback loops to drive continuous operational improvement across the institution.

Chapter One: INTRODUCTION 1.1 Background Makerere University Business School, in line with Government policy and national public service reforms, is mandated to develop and implement Service Delivery Standards (SDS) to guide institutional service delivery and promote accountability, efficiency, and quality in the provision of services. In fulfilment of this mandate, MUBS has developed the SDSs for the Financial Years 2025/2026 to 2029/2030 to provide a clear framework for service delivery across the institution. According to the Ministry of Public Service, Service Delivery Standards are “specific descriptions of the level of service that an organisation seeks to provide to its clients and stakeholders.” They define the minimum acceptable standards in terms of quality, timeliness, accessibility, cost, quantity, and responsiveness in the delivery of services. At MUBS, the SDSs provide clearly defined benchmarks that guide the quality, timeliness, efficiency, consistency, and professionalism of services provided across all academic, administrative, and support functions of the institution. The standards establish measurable expectations for both service providers and service users, ensuring that institutional processes are predictable, transparent, accountable, and aligned with agreed performance levels. The development of the SDSs is also aligned with the MUBS Strategic Plan 2025–2030 and is intended to support the achievement of the institution’s vision, mission, and strategic objectives. The standards, therefore, serve as an important management and accountability tool for strengthening institutional performance, improving stakeholder satisfaction, and promoting a culture of continuous improvement and service excellence. Through these standards, MUBS aims to build stakeholder confidence, improve coordination in service

delivery, strengthen institutional monitoring and evaluation, and ensure that all clients receive fair, timely, and high-quality services across the institution.  1.2 Rationale and Purpose of SDS As MUBS continues to grow and its services become more diverse, the importance of establishing clear standards becomes more important. The next section presents the objectives of SDSs and how these standards translate into practical goals that strengthen service delivery at MUBS. 1.3 Mandate of MUBS MUBS is a Public Tertiary Institution established through a merger of Makerere University’s Faculty of Commerce and the National College of Business Studies. It is governed by the Universities and Other Tertiary Institutions Act Cap. 262 (Act) and the regulations made thereunder. The Act in section 136 creates MUBS as a public tertiary institute affiliated with Makerere University, and in section 71(2) saves the structures and organs of the governance of MUBS as established under the Makerere University (Establishment of Constituent College) Order, 1997. The Act empowers MUBS to formulate its own statutes, regulations, policies, and administrative structures for the overall administration and ensure due implementation of the functions and objects of MUBS.  The object for which MUBS was established is to provide instruction and advance knowledge in commerce and business through: i) Undergraduate, postgraduate, higher diploma, and ordinary diploma courses.

Chapter Two: THE FRAMEWORK OF THE MUBS SERVICE DELIVERY STANDARDS 2.1 Objectives of the service delivery standards MUBS SDSs are intended to achieve the following objectives: i) To define the minimum level of services that MUBS should provide and clearly state what clients should expect in terms of quality, quantity, time, cost, accessibility and coverage. This gives a clear basis for judging service performance. ii) Ensure consistency in service delivery across all units of MUBS so that similar services are delivered in the same way and at the same standard, regardless of where they are offered. iii) Empower service users, including students, staff, and other stakeholders, by making service expectations clear. This enables them to request and expect services that meet agreed standards. iv) Support quality control by guiding staff on how services should be delivered and ensuring that service delivery is regularly checked and improved. v) Ensure that daily operations are aligned with the MUBS Strategic Plan so that all activities contribute directly to institutional goals and priorities. To better understand their practical value, results are expected when these standards are implemented and the expected outcomes are presented in the next section. 2.2 Expected Outcomes of Implementing SDS MUBS is expected to improve service delivery and experience for all stakeholders. CONCLUSION The MUBS SDSs mark a major step in strengthening the quality, consistency, and accountability of service delivery across MUBS. They reflect the commitment of MUBS to providing services that are timely, professional, transparent, and responsive to the needs of students, staff, partners, and other stakeholders. These Standards represent a shared commitment to uphold the values and principles that define MUBS as an institution of excellence. They provide a clear understanding of the level of service stakeholders should expect and the responsibility of every member of the MUBS community in achieving those standards. The successful implementation of the SDSs will depend on the collective efforts and teamwork of management, staff, students, and stakeholders working together towards a common goal of improving institutional performance and stakeholder satisfaction. Through its core values of professionalism, teamwork, innovation, responsiveness, and effective use of technology, MUBS commits to strengthening its culture of quality service delivery and continuous improvement. As MUBS moves forward in implementing these Standards, the institution reaffirms its commitment to academic excellence, effective governance, innovation, and stakeholder-centered service delivery. The SDSs therefore provide a framework for service delivery and a foundation for building a stronger, more responsive, and future-oriented institution.
